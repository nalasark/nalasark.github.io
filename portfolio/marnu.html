Marnu:
AR Restaurant Menu

Augmented Reality UX Prototyping

The Problem

Restaurant menus are a visual source of information that directly affects the food choices customers make, hence their satisfaction level. At times, customers end up making bad, uninformed choices due to lack of visual information.

We propose an AR-enabled menu experience that allows users to properly visualise the dishes on the menu with real size 3D models and detailed information about each dish to make a more informed choice for the meal.

Prototypes

Throughout the research, we created several prototypes with different approaches to understand what works best with users in an AR space. Below are 2 prototypes that I was involved in creating, which are constrasting in 2 aspects.

Floating Table Prototype

The floating table concept is designed to follow the traditional restaurant tablet menu flow, to maintain a sense of familiarity for users, but redesigned for an AR, 3D space.

The floating table allows customers to preview each menu item in real size, before swiping it down to the dining table, which also serves as a life-sized preview of their cart.

Physical Display Prototype

Unlike the floating table, the physical display menu is presented in a 3D menu anchored in AR space. The interactivity here is designed for AR, unlike existing tablet menus.

The adding, removing and previewing of menu items are all integrated into the life-sized cart preview. Users will always see their cart in real-size while making more choices.
